## Why is 450 °C used in the Haber process?

1. higher temperature gives a higher rate (e.g. Particles move faster, so collisions are more frequent and more successful.)
2. higher temperature shifts the position of equilibrium to the left (e.g. Because ammonia formation is exothermic, higher temperature favours the backward reaction.)
3. higher temperature uses more energy so increases costs
4. the temperature chosen is a compromise between rate of reaction and position of equilibrium (e.g. 450 °C is not the best for yield alone or rate alone; it balances both.)
