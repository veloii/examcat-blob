## How do you make pure dry crystals of a soluble salt by reacting a dilute acid with an insoluble metal oxide or carbonate?

1. warm the acid (e.g. Warm gently, not to boiling.)
2. add the insoluble solid
3. continue adding until the solid is in excess (e.g. Excess means some solid is left over.)
4. filter to remove excess solid
5. heat the filtrate or solution to crystallisation point (e.g. Stop when the solution is concentrated enough for crystals to start forming on cooling.)
6. leave to cool and crystallise
7. pat the crystals dry with filter paper (e.g. This removes solution stuck to the crystals.)

## How do you complete a titration?

1. add the solution until there is a permanent colour change (e.g. Permanent means the colour stays changed after swirling.)
2. take or record the burette reading
3. add the solution dropwise near the end-point
4. swirl
5. use a white tile
6. repeat and calculate a mean (e.g. Use concordant results when finding the mean.)
