Investigate reflection of light by different types of surface and refraction of light by different substances.

### Indicative content

- Place a glass block on a piece of paper.
- Draw around the glass block.
- Use the ray box to shine a ray of light through the glass block.
- Mark the ray of light entering the glass block.
- Mark the ray of light emerging from the glass block.
- Join the points to show the complete path of the ray through the block.
- Draw a normal line at 90° to the surface.
- Use a protractor to measure the angle of incidence.
- Use a protractor to measure the angle of refraction.
- Use the ray box to shine a ray at a range of different angles of incidence.
- Increase the angle of incidence in 10° intervals.
- Use angles of incidence from 10° to 60°.
- Labelled diagrams could contain some of the indicative content.
- Methods involving mirrors and reflection scored zero for this refraction question.

### Extra mark scheme points from nearby questions

- A wider ray is less accurate because it is harder to judge/mark the centre of the ray, causing greater uncertainty or random error.
- For reflection, the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
- Variation in repeated reflection angles is a random error; causes can include parallax, marking the centre of the ray incorrectly, or not replacing the mirror/ray box/protractor correctly.
- If the ray travels along the normal, refraction may not occur because the wavefront enters the glass at the same time / along the normal.
- A protractor with 1° resolution should not be used to record angles to 1 decimal place.
- For refraction data, the line of best fit may be a curve, not a straight line.

## Specific examples

- Refraction apparatus: ray box, glass block, paper, pencil, ruler, protractor.
- Reflection apparatus: ray box, mirror or test surface, paper, protractor.
- Always measure angles from the normal, not from the surface.
- Mark two points on each visible ray so the ray path can be joined accurately with a ruler.
- Do not answer a refraction method question with a mirror/reflection method unless the question explicitly asks for reflection.
