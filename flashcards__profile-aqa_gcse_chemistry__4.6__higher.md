## How are economical conditions chosen for industrial equilibrium processes?

- chosen temperature is a compromise between rate and yield (e.g. A higher temperature gives a faster rate, but may lower yield in exothermic processes.)
- chosen pressure is a compromise between yield / rate and cost (e.g. A higher pressure can improve yield and rate, but is expensive.)
- higher temperatures use more energy so costs increase
- higher pressures use more energy so costs increase
- higher pressures require stronger reaction vessels so costs increase (e.g. Industrial equipment must be strong enough to withstand high pressure.)
