## How is atom economy calculated?

1. calculate the total Mr of the reactants
2. divide the Mr of the desired product by the total Mr of the reactants and multiply by 100 (e.g. Use the Mr of the useful product only, not all products.)

## How do you calculate moles from a gas volume at RTP?

1. convert gas volume to dm3 or use 24 000 cm3 for 1 mole (e.g. At RTP, 1 mole of any gas occupies 24 dm3 or 24 000 cm3.)
2. moles = volume ÷ 24 (e.g. Use ÷24 if volume is in dm3, or ÷24 000 if it is in cm3.)

## How do you calculate gas volume from moles at RTP?

1. find the number of moles of gas (e.g. You may need to get moles from a mass or from the equation first.)
2. volume = moles × 24 dm3 (e.g. At RTP, every mole of gas has a volume of 24 dm3.)

## How do you identify the limiting reactant?

1. calculate moles of each reactant
2. use the balanced equation to find the amount needed of one reactant for the other (e.g. Compare what you have with what the equation says is needed.)
3. the reactant present in insufficient amount is the limiting reactant (e.g. It runs out first, so it stops more product forming.)

## What are the steps for calculating concentration in a titration?

2. use the balanced equation to find moles of the other substance (e.g. Take the mole ratio from the balanced equation.)
3. calculate concentration using moles ÷ volume in dm3 (e.g. Use the unknown solution's volume in dm3.)
