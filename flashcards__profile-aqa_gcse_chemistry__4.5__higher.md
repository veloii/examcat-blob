## How do you calculate overall energy change from bond energies?

1. calculate the energy needed for bonds broken
2. calculate the energy released in bonds made
3. overall energy change = energy for bonds broken − energy for bonds made (e.g. broken − made)

## How can temperature change be used to compare the reactivity of metals?

1. add each metal to the same reacting solution (e.g. Use the same solution for each metal.)
2. measure the temperature before and after reaction
3. the greater the temperature change, the more reactive the metal (e.g. A bigger temperature rise means a more reactive metal.)
4. keep control variables the same (e.g. Keep volume, concentration, metal amount, particle size and starting temperature the same.)

## How can the accuracy of temperature-change measurements be improved?

- insulate the container (e.g. This reduces heat transfer to or from the surroundings.)
- add a lid (e.g. A lid reduces heat loss, especially from the top.)
- repeat the experiment and calculate a mean
- measure the highest temperature reached (e.g. For endothermic reactions, use the lowest temperature reached.)
