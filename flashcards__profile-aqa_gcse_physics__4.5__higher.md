## Why does one object slow down in a collision?

1. (total) momentum is conserved in the collision (e.g. If external forces are negligible.)
2. the momentum of the other object increases (e.g. Momentum is transferred to the other object.)
3. so the momentum of the original object decreases
4. so its velocity decreases (e.g. If its mass stays the same, lower momentum means lower velocity.)
