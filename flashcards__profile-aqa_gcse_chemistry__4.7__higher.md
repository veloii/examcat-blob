## How does fractional distillation separate crude oil?

1. crude oil is heated to vaporise (the hydrocarbons) (e.g. Heating turns many hydrocarbons in crude oil into vapour.)
2. there is a temperature gradient in the (fractionating) column (e.g. The fractionating column is hottest at the bottom and coolest at the top.)
3. vapours / gases condense (e.g. As vapours rise, they condense at different levels in the column.)
4. fractions have different boiling points (e.g. They separate because each fraction has a different boiling point.)

## How does boiling point change across the alkanes, and why?

1. the boiling point increases as the number of (carbon) atoms increases (e.g. Longer-chain alkanes have higher boiling points than shorter-chain alkanes.)
2. the weak intermolecular forces increase (e.g. More energy is needed to overcome the intermolecular forces.)
3. these intermolecular forces increase as the size of the molecules increases (e.g. Bigger molecules have stronger intermolecular forces than smaller ones.)
